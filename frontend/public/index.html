About Me

Greetings! I'm Sreekanth Netha Thatikonda.

I am a passionate Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering graduate student from Arizona State University. My academic journey has been an exciting exploration of both theoretical knowledge and practical application. My fascination with engineering started during my undergraduate studies, where I developed a keen interest in data science through a project involving fluid conveyance negotiating in different mediums and analyzing the effects using multiple software tools. This experience ignited my passion for design, architecture, and data analysis, motivating me to delve deeper into this interdisciplinary field.

I have honed my skills in various programming languages such as Python, R, and MATLAB, along with proficiency in design, simulation, and system tools. My professional experience includes working as a Hydraulics Systems Engineer at CNH Industrial, where I have been involved in the design, development, and detailing of hydraulic and pneumatic components and systems for skid steers and compact track loaders.

As a Graduate Teaching Assistant at Arizona State University, I have supported advanced topics in science, programming languages, and design, helping students navigate complex subjects and practical applications.

I am eager to leverage my engineering expertise to further my professional growth. I am excited to take on new challenges, implement innovative solutions, and contribute meaningfully to a dynamic team.
